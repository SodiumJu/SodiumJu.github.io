Reinforcement Learning in Games

ResTNet

Although AlphaZero has achieved superhuman performance in board games, recent studies reveal its limitations in handling scenarios requiring a comprehensive understanding of the entire board, such as recognizing long-sequence patterns in Go. To address this challenge, we propose ResTNet, a network that interleaves residual and Transformer blocks to bridge local and global knowledge.

ResTNet improves playing strength across multiple board games, increasing win rate significantly in 9x9 Go, 19x19 Go, and Hex. In addition, ResTNet effectively processes global information and tackles long-sequence patterns like circular and ladder patterns in Go.

Bioinformatics Research

LinearCDSfold

Currently, a dynamic programming (DP) algorithm CDSfold has been proposed to design a CDS by minimizing the minimum free energy (MFE) of its secondary structure. However, it has been questioned recently that such a DP algorithm is difficult to be modified to design a CDS when attempting to jointly optimize its secondary structure stability and codon adaptation index (CAI).

In this study, we successfully modify the DP algorithm of CDSfold to exactly solve this kind of CDS design problem in O(L³) time and O(L²) space. We further accelerate this algorithm by beam search, enabling it to design high-quality approximate CDS in O(L) time, implemented as LinearCDSfold.

Bioinformatics Research

Multi-CSAR

Multi-CSAR is an efficient scaffolder designed to utilize multiple reference genomes to improve the quality of genomic assemblies. By interpreting relationships between multiple related genomes, it provides a more accurate and contiguous representation of the target genome.

This research addresses the challenges of fragmented assemblies in genome sequencing, showing that leveraging evolutionary conservation across multiple references can significantly enhance scaffolding performance and reliability.
